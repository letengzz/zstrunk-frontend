## FAQ

Q: What do I need to know about driving a dump truck?

A: Never overload the upper portion of the truck bed.
Dump on level ground.
Avoid soft surfaces.
Do not exit the cab while dumping.
Never dump near people or other vehicles.

Q: How much fuel does a dump truck use per day?

A: A dump truck typically uses between 415 to 500 gallons of fuel per week, or roughly 59 to 71 gallons per day, depending on factors like mileage and fuel efficiency. Dump trucks generally get around 5 to 6 miles per gallon.

Q: What is the load carrying capacity range of HOWO dump trucks?

A: The load carrying capacity of HOWO dump trucks typically ranges from 25 to 60 tons, making them suitable for different transportation requirements.

Q: What is the fuel consumption of a HOWO dump truck?

A: The fuel consumption varies by tonnage. It can be up to 35 L/100 km for 25 - ton models, 40 L/100 km for 30 - ton models, 45 L/100 km for 40 - ton models, and 50 L/100 km for 60 - ton models.

Q: How is the driving comfort of HOWO dump trucks?

A: The cab is spacious and ergonomic, with adjustable seats, ample storage compartments, and user - friendly controls. It also has features like air conditioning, heating, ventilation, and excellent sound insulation. The cabin damping system reduces vibrations, and there is a sleeping area designed according to European standards, with a comfortable bed for rest.

Q: What is the life expectancy of a dump truck?

A: The dump truck can stay operational for about 10 to 20 years. While this is indeed a rough estimate, the longevity heavily depends on the truck's maintenance history.
