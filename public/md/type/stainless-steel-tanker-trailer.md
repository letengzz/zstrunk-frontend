## Specialized Linings in Stainless Steel Tanker Trailers

**Epoxy Linings**

Epoxy linings are commonly applied to the interior of chemical tanker tanks. They provide an additional layer of protection against chemical reactions and corrosion. Epoxy linings are designed to be compatible with specific chemicals and cargoes.

**Rubber or plastic linings**

Rubber or plastic linings are used for tanks transporting more aggressive or corrosive chemicals. These linings create a barrier between the cargo and the tank material, preventing chemical reactions. Proper selection of lining material is essential to ensuring compatibility with the transported chemicals.

## FAQ

Q: How do you clean a stainless steel tanker?

A: Stainless Steel's best friends are quite simply soap, mild detergent or ammonia solutions in warm water, applied with a soft cloth or nylon sponge. Occasionally the use of the least coarse nylon scouring pad may be required. Rinse and dry with a soft cloth.

Q: How much does a stainless tanker weigh?

A: Stainless steel tankers are at the other end of the spectrum. Depending on their capacity, stainless chemical tankers can weigh anywhere from 12,000 to 15,000 pounds.

Q: Are tanker trailers double walled?

A: Designed to meet safety standards set by DOT, these trailers are equipped with features such as double-wall construction, emergency venting, and spill containment systems to ensure the safe transport of hazardous materials.

Q: What is the average size of a tanker trailer?

A: Tanker trailers, flatbeds and a variety of other trailer types all range between 48-53 feet. Extendable double drops and Removable Goosenecks (RGN) are some of the longest trailers available. Both reach 80 feet long when fully extended and typically carry other vehicles and large equipment.

Q: What MPG does a semi get?

A: Semi-trucks typically get around 6.5 to 7.5 miles per gallon (MPG). This is significantly lower than the average car, which gets around 25 MPG. The lower fuel efficiency of semi-trucks is primarily due to their heavy weight and the aerodynamic drag they experience.

Q: How far can a semi go on empty?

A: A semi-truck's range on empty heavily depends on its fuel tank capacity and mileage. Semi-trucks typically have fuel tanks ranging from 200 to 300 gallons and achieve an average fuel economy of 6.5 to 8 miles per gallon. Therefore, a semi-truck could potentially travel between 1,300 and 2,400 miles before running out of fuel.
