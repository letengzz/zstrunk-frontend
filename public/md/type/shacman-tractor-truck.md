Cargo loading and fixing are important factors affecting the driving safety of Shacman Tractor Truck. When loading cargo, drivers should ensure that the cargo is evenly distributed, the center of gravity is stable, and the cargo is firmly fixed with appropriate fixing devices. Avoid overloading or partial loading to reduce driving risks.

Shacman Tractor Truck drivers should be extra careful when passing through bridges, tunnels, mountainous areas and other special road sections. These road sections may have height and width restrictions, drivers should know the road conditions in advance and make the corresponding driving plan.

**Maintain good driving habits**

Good driving habits are essential to ensure driving safety, and Shacman Tractor Truck drivers should maintain good habits such as concentration, no fatigue driving, and no drunk driving. At the same time, pay attention to weather changes. Bad weather conditions (such as fog, heavy rain, ice and snow) pose a serious threat to the driving safety of tractor-trailer trucks.

**Strictly abide by traffic rules**

Compliance with traffic rules is the basic premise of driving safety, Shacman Tractor Truck drivers should strictly abide by traffic signals, speed limit signs and other regulations, do not run red lights, and do not speed to ensure the safety of themselves and others.

**Regularly check the condition of the vehicle**

Vehicle condition directly affects driving safety, Shacman Tractor Truck drivers should regularly inspect and maintain the vehicle, including the engine, brake system, tires and other key components. **Maintain Safe Distance and Speed**

Due to the Shacman Tractor Truck's large size and strong load capacity, its braking distance is relatively long. Drivers should maintain a sufficient safety distance in the driving process, so that in case of emergency there is enough time and space for braking or avoiding.

**Responding to Emergencies**
